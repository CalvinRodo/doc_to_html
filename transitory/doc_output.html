    A Labour Market Benefits Plan (LMBP) shows an employer\u2019s commitment to creating positive impacts on the\xa0labour\xa0market. To ensure that your LMBP does this, you must work with Service Canada\xa0to:\xa0

    • develop activities to meet commitments\xa0
    • identify commitments,\xa0and\xa0
    • track the progress of commitments\xa0

    What to include in an LMBP\xa0

    LMBP commitments are split into two categories:\xa0

    • 1 mandatory benefit\xa0
    • At least 2 complementary benefits\xa0

    Service Canada\xa0will work with employers to complete the LMBP during the Global Talent Stream (GTS) application process.\xa0

    Identify the mandatory benefit\xa0

    For\xa0the\xa0mandatory benefit(s)\xa0in their LMBP, every employer under the GTS must identify at least 1 activity that will support their achievement of the benefit.\xa0

    The employer\xa0must\xa0also\xa0identify at least 2 complementary benefits for which they are committing to achieving as part of their LMBP. The complementary benefits identified by an employer in their\xa0plan\xa0 cannot\xa0be the same \xa0as the mandatory benefit identified.\xa0\xa0

    Category A of the GTS\xa0

    Employers must commit to \xa0job creation for Canadians and permanent residents \u202fas your mandatory benefit. For this benefit, employers will need to track the number of jobs either directly connected to the temporary foreign worker (TFW) hired or the overall job growth within the company.\xa0

    Example:\xa0

    Activities to support this benefit could include, but are not limited to:\xa0

    • Hiring additional Canadians and permanent residents to support the foreign worker in their role; and\xa0
    • Increasing the number of Canadians or permanent residents employed full-time and part-time by the employer\xa0
    end
    end
    Category B of the GTS\xa0

    Employers must commit to investments \xa0in skills and training for Canadians and permanent residents as your mandatory benefit .\xa0\xa0

    Example\xa0

    Activities to support this benefit could include, but are not limited to:\xa0

    • Increasing\xa0financial\xa0investments in internal skills and training.\xa0
    • Establishing partnerships with post-secondary institutes or other organizations to support skills and training. For example, providing them with free licenses or access to specialized software that will help build student skills.\xa0
    • Providing paid co-op or internship opportunities at the firm, and\xa0
    • Participating in work-integrated learning or other federal and provincial skills and training programs.\xa0
    end
    end

    There are many activities that can support your mandatory benefit and 2 complementary benefits.\xa0\xa0

    Here are some examples of activities for each benefit:\xa0

    • Hiring additional Canadians or permanent residents to support the TFW in their role; and\u202f\xa0

    Progress reviews\xa0

    Each year,\xa0Service\xa0Canada\xa0will conduct progress reviews to assess how well employers are meeting the commitments detailed in your LMBP. The annual reviews are employers\u2019 opportunity to report on the progress that the company is making in creating lasting, positive impacts on the Canadian\xa0labour\xa0market.\xa0

    Cooperating with\xa0 Service Canada\xa0throughout the review process is important. Employers must provide\xa0supporting documentation\xa0to\xa0remain eligible. You must keep your records for\u202f six full years \u202ffor audit purposes.\xa0

    If\xa0you\xa0do not make reasonable efforts to meet your LMBP commitments\xa0

    If\xa0you\xa0do not make reasonable efforts to meet your LMBP commitments, or complete your Progress Review within the requested timeframe, you risk receiving a negative decision on future GTS applications. Your LMBP\u2019s assessment will not impact your eligibility to other program streams.\xa0

    You are accountable for the benefits in your LMBP even if a TFW you hire under the GTS becomes a permanent resident of Canada while working in your organization.\xa0

    end

    Making changes to the LMBP\xa0

    Employers\xa0who have\xa0an approved LMBP and apply for additional TFW positions\xa0through the\xa0GTS,\xa0\xa0will\xa0not have to submit a new LMBP, however,\xa0 Service Canada\xa0will review your existing plan as a result.\xa0
