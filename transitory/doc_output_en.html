How we determine compensation\xa0

Employers\xa0must\xa0offer the\xa0temporary foreign worker(s)\xa0(TFW)\xa0the\xa0same level and form of compensation as Canadians and permanent residents performing the same\xa0job\xa0with similar skills and experience\xa0at the same location.\xa0

Employers must offer\xa0and pay TFWs\xa0the higher of:\xa0

Same wages as existing employees \xa0

The wages the employer pays its domestic employees for the same job, work location and with similar skills and years of experience;\xa0\xa0

If no comparable position exists in your organization\xa0

If no comparable position exists in your organization, employers must pay workers at least the median wage according to the position\u2019s\xa0National Occupational Classification\xa0( NOC)\xa0Code \xa0and work\xa0location\xa0and publicly available\xa0on Job Bank.\xa0

Median wage of the NOC on Job Bank \xa0

The median wage of the NOC and work location as provided on Job Bank;\xa0\xa0

  • Determine the median and wage range in Job Bank\xa0

To determine the median and wage range in Job Bank:\xa0

  • go to\u202f Compare wages on Job Bank \xa0
  • in the \u201cJob search\u201d field, enter the job title or the\xa0National Occupational Classification\xa0(NOC)\u202fthat best describes the job duties and requirements\xa0
  • in the middle column will be the hourly median wage, by community or area. If listed as \u201cN/A'', consult the provincial or territorial wage. If it is not available, consult the national wage\xa0

In your application, you must identify the high and low wage for the position, based on the NOC and work location on Job Bank. You must use the most up-to-date wage range on Job Bank at the time of your application.\xa0

Wage\xa0defined by Labour Market Information \xa0

The wage as defined by other publicly available and credible\xa0labour\xa0market information.\xa0\xa0

Wage compensation does not include overtime hours, tips, benefits, profit sharing, bonuses, commissions, or company shares.\xa0

Note: \u202fService Canada may modify the\xa0National Occupational Classification\xa0(NOC)\xa0selected to best reflect job duties of the position, which may result in an increase to the minimum wage requirements.\xa0

  • advertise and offer the same wage rates as those established under the collective agreement\u202f\xa0
  • offer the\u202fTFW\u202fthe same terms and conditions as Canadian and permanent resident\xa0workers\u202f The\xa0hiring of TFWs\u202fmust not\u202faffect current or future\xa0labour\xa0disputes.\xa0
