As an employer applying through the Global Talent Stream (GTS) you must comply with the Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP) and the GTS requirements.

You must also:

  • show that your business and job offer are legitimate
  • uphold the conditions and rules listed in the Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations (IRPR) and the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA)
  • participate in Progress Reviews of your Labour Market Benefits Plan (LMBP)

You may also need to provide a Labour Market Impact Asessment (LMIA). Find out if you need an LMIA .

The TFWP will check that you are meeting the program requirements . Employers who do not follow these requirements may face consequences.

Category A

Your company is eligible for Category A of the GTS only if you are referred by one of the stream’s designated referral partners.

The referral partner will check that your company meets the following criteria:

  • is operating in Canada
  • has a focus on innovation
  • is able and willing to grow and scale up
  • fills a unique and specialized position in the company
  • has identified a qualified foreign worker for a unique and specialized position
  • any additional criteria implemented by the Designated Referral Partner for members of its organization

Unique and specialized positions

Most often those eligible for Category A will have 1 or 2 unique and specialized positions per calendar year.

A unique and specialized position is indicated by:

  • a minimum wage of $38.46 per hour ($80,000 annual base salary), or;
  • equivalent to the median wage of the National Occupations Classification (NOC) and work location as provided on Job Bank , if it is higher
  • Employers must offer the equivalent of both the hourly wage rate and the annual base salary, at minimum

The position must require:

  • advanced knowledge of the industry, and
  • an advanced degree in an area of specialization of interest to the employer, and/or
  • a minimum of 5 years of experience in the field of specialized experience

Service Canada may consider accepting more than 2 referrals for an employer per calendar year only if the following conditions are met:

  • the employer has been successfully meeting its LMBP commitments to date
  • the referral partner has re-confirmed the employer’s eligibility for Category A
  • the annual wage for the additional position is:
  • at least $72.11 per hour ($150,000 annual base salary) or;
  • equivalent to the median wage of the NOC and work location as provided on Job Bank , if it is higher

Please note that Service Canada does not allow exceptions on wage criteria for additional positions in the cases where the annual salary of the first or second referral was at or above $150,000.

Category B

Category B is for employers in Canada that need to fill vacant positions found on the Global Talent Occupations List. You do not need a referral to apply to Category B

Some occupations on the Global Talent Occupations List have additional eligibility criteria.


Global Talent Occupations List for Category B occupations

Unless additional wage requirements are listed below, all positions must be paid a wage equal or greater to the prevailing wage for the National Occupations Classification (NOC) code, based on the economic region for the location of work. See Compensation page for more information.
